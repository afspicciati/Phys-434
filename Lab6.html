b) Is your observed signal biased? (e.g. is the observed value equally likely to be stronger or weaker than the true injected signal?) Is it symmetric?¶

e) Discuss the answer to part d in some depth. Is it symmetric? Is it biased? Does this make sense?¶

This means that there is a 67% chance that our observed signal is $\pm 10$ from the true signal that is observed signal minus background. This makes sense because both the injected signal and background vary. So there are many injected signals that could produce the same value of observed data, and vice versa. It is symmetric, roughly, because the poisson distribution the data is pulled from has a high mean (100) and nears to a gaussian distribution due to this and the central limit theorem. It is also biased, because of the shift in data.

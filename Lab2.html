def convolve(dist,n=2,mode='full'):
    if n == 1:
        return dist
    convolution = signal.convolve(dist,dist,mode=mode)
    for a in range(n-2):
        convolution = signal.convolve(convolution,dist,mode=mode)
    return convolution

Here I show that after 5 days, the summed probability distribution is equivalent to a poisson distribution centered at 5x the expected value per night. The second plot shows that the difference in the two lines is nearly 0 (to a factor of 10^-15) for all values k. This makes sense conceptually, because the results of one night have no impact on the results of the next.

C)¶

When you average the summed probability distributions, the resulting distribution instead shifts to be closer to the expected value of k, and narrower as you increase the number of days. This is because as you take data over more days, the liklihood of getting outlier values over and over becomes increasingly low. This result means the tails of the distribution become more symmetric over time, and seems to tend towards a gaussian. The Central Limit Theorem says that many distributions when summed and averaged over enough iterations will tend towards the Normal Distribution. This appears to be true of the poisson distribution, but as it is discrete, it can never actually become a gaussian.
